I will join Department of Information Engineering at Chinese University of Hong Kong as a Research Assistant Professor in Aug 2019.

I have passed my dissertation defense after 3.5 years at School of Information Systems, Singapore Management University (SMU).

My PhD study was generously supported by the SMU's Secure Mobile Center (SMC) PhD Scholarship (the only awardee).

I am working closely with my two PhD advisors, Prof. Debin Gao and Prof. Robert H. Deng, on a number of cool security projects.

I also get chance to collaborate with Prof. Yingjiu Li, Prof. David Lo, and Prof. Lingxiao Jiang on Android security or software engineering.

My MPhil advisor at HK PolyU was Prof. Rocky K. C. Chang, with whom I am currently working on mobile network measurement.


Research interests: Mobile Security; Program Analysis; Network Measurement; App Crowdsourcing; Smart Contract.


I am leading the design and implementation of the following crowdsourcing projects. Please consider trying them:

  • MopEye measures the per-app network speed, and many more (capture packets, change DNS). [Homepage | Dataset]

  • NetMon scans your 3G/4G/WiFi networks, and monitors open ports in your device. [Homepage]

Mentoring Experience

  • AppTrace: Dynamic Trace on Android Devices

    The paper appeared in ICC 2015, IEEE International Conference on Communications, London, UK.

    The idea, a "powerful" on-device method tracing system for Android, was proposed by me in 2013. This makes a preliminary version.

    I mentored the first two authors to develop the current AppTrace system based on Xposed. But I did not involve in the writing process.
